Software and art

In 2019, I founded re|thread, a collective of software and art researchers, in Stockholm. Since then, I have been involved in a number of different activities at the intersection of software and art: design and exhibit software art with re|thread; teach algorithmic art; collaborate on the folkfigur NFT project; restore digital artworks; develop my own practice of generative art.

Algorithmic art course

Since 2024, I teach the algorithmic art course at Université de Montréal. It is a graduate level course, opened to students in computer science, art history and design. It introduces students to the practices of sofware as an artistic medium, to open source software and to the cultural aspects of software and code.

Folkfigur NFT collection

In 2022, I collaborated on the first ever NFT project commissioned by a public art institution. The Swedish Arts Council commissioned Ilja Karilampi to develop a NFT collection for the Swedish people. Together with Martin Monperrus, we designed and developed the software concept for the Folkfigur NFT collection.
